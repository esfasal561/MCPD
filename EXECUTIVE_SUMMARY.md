# Executive Summary – MCPD (Magnetically Captured Plasma Drive)

The MCPD propulsion concept introduces a hybrid architecture combining a nuclear thermal kick stage with a high‑efficiency plasma engine capable of capturing and accelerating interplanetary plasma. This approach drastically reduces propellant mass while enabling continuous thrust and high cumulative Δv.

The system uses a magnetic intake to collect solar wind and interplanetary plasma, a toroidal confinement chamber for stabilization, RF heating for ion energization, and a magnetic nozzle for efficient plasma detachment.

**Key advantages:**
- High specific impulse (~5,000 s)
- Continuous thrust (25–50 N)
- Reduced propellant mass
- Scalable architecture
- Suitable for deep‑space missions

**Mission impact:**
The MCPD enables Earth–Mars transfers in 3–5 months with significant Δv margins for braking and orbital insertion. It represents a promising direction for fast interplanetary transport and long‑duration exploration.
